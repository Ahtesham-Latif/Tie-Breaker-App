# 📖 The TieBreaker - User Manual

Welcome to **The TieBreaker**! We built this tool because we believe making big decisions shouldn't mean drowning in endless search results or generic AI chatbot answers.

Whether you're a student picking a major, a developer choosing a tech stack, or an entrepreneur deciding on a business strategy, this guide will walk you through how to use the app to get the best possible results when you're stuck between two tough choices.

---

* **👔 Managers & Entrepreneurs:** Making strategic business moves (`In-house Team` vs `Outsourcing`, `LLC` vs `S-Corp`) or evaluating project management tools (`Jira` vs `Linear`).

## 1. The Core Interface (The Decision Engine)

When you open the app, you'll see a clean, distraction-free dashboard. Here is how to use the main inputs:

### Option A vs Option B
* **What it is:** The two main text boxes at the top of your screen.
* **How to use it:** Enter exactly what you are trying to decide between. 
* *Example:* `Next.js` vs `Vite` or `Mailchimp` vs `ConvertKit`.

### Comparison Factors (Optional)
* **What it is:** The section where you can click **"Add Option"** to define what matters most to your specific role.
* **How to use it:** Add single-word or short-phrase criteria you want the AI to judge the options by.
* *Example for Devs:* `Performance`, `Bundle Size`, `Learning Curve`.
* *Example for Marketers:* `ROI`, `Ease of Use`, `Integration capabilities`.
* *Tip:* If you don't add any factors, our intelligent engine will automatically generate the most logical factors for you!

### Deep Research Toggle
* **What it is:** A simple on/off switch labeled **"Deep Research"**.
* **How to use it:** Turn this ON if your options involve volatile information like current pricing, real-time market data, or recent software updates. When ON, the AI will actively search the web before answering. Keep it OFF for general philosophical or historical comparisons to get faster results.

### "My Case" / Personalized Context (Member Only)
* **What it is:** A secure text box allowing up to 500 characters of personal context. 
* **How to use it:** You must have an account to unlock this feature! This is where you tell the AI *who you are* and *why you are deciding*. 
* *Example (Student):* "I am a college student on a tight budget studying graphic design. I need something lightweight because I commute on the train every day."
* *Example (Entrepreneur):* "I run a 5-person startup and we need a CRM that scales cheaply for the first year."
* *Why it matters:* This context completely changes the "Final Verdict." Without it, the AI gives a generic winner. With it, the AI picks the winner *specifically for your exact situation*.

## 2. The Analysis Tabs

Once you hit **"Calculate Analysis"**, the AI engine processes your dilemma and unlocks four powerful tabs at the top of your screen.

### ⚖️ Comparison
* **The Role:** The objective facts.
* **What it shows:** A side-by-side table breaking down both options across all your requested factors. It strips away opinions and just gives you the raw data in an easy-to-read grid.

### 👍 Pros & Cons
* **The Role:** The highlight reel.
* **What it shows:** A rapid-fire list of the best and worst attributes of each option. Perfect for scanning quickly to see if there are any immediate dealbreakers.

### 🎯 SWOT Analysis
* **The Role:** The deep business-style evaluation.
* **What it shows:** A grid detailing the **S**trengths, **W**eaknesses, **O**pportunities, and **T**hreats for each option. 
* *When to use it:* Highly recommended when you are making business, financial, or strategic decisions (e.g., Managers choosing a software vendor or Entrepreneurs deciding between two go-to-market strategies).

### 🏆 Final Verdict
* **The Role:** The definitive answer.
* **What it shows:** The AI takes all the data from the previous tabs, heavily weighs it against your **"My Case"** input, and explicitly declares a winner. It explains exactly *why* it won and offers a brief concluding thought.

## 3. Navigation & Hidden Features

To keep the interface clean, some features use minimalistic icons.

### 💡 Tooltips 
* **Desktop:** Simply hover your mouse over any icon button (like the Copy icon or Theme Toggle) to see a tooltip explaining what it does.
* **Mobile:** Tap and hold your finger on an icon for half a second. A custom, animated tooltip will pop up to guide you!

### 🌗 Dark Mode / Light Mode
* **Where to find it:** When viewing your results, you'll find the Theme Toggle (Sun/Moon icon) sitting perfectly in the top-left corner, just above the analysis header.
* **How it works:** Tap it instantly to swap between our beautiful Dark and Light color palettes without losing any of your data.

### 🗂️ Split vs. Stack View
* **Where to find it:** Inside the results panel header, there is a `Split/Stack` icon (Columns/Rows).
* **How it works:** This allows you to toggle the layout of your Pros/Cons and Comparisons from a side-by-side view (Split) to a vertically stacked view (Stack). Stacked is often easier to read on mobile devices!

## 4. Account Features & History

The TieBreaker is free to try, but creating an account unlocks powerful persistence.

### My History Sidebar
* **The Role:** Your personal decision vault.
* **How it works:** If you are logged in, every dilemma you generate is securely saved to the cloud. You can click the **My History** button on the left sidebar to open your history.
* **Clicking a past decision:** When you click an old decision, it instantly loads your past data onto the screen without making you wait for the AI to generate it again!

### The Free Tier (Usage Limits)
* **Anonymous Users:** You can use the engine up to 3 times for free. After that, you'll see a friendly prompt asking you to create an account.
* **Registered Users:** Your free account grants you up to 15 full AI TieBreaker decisions.

## 💡 Best Practices for Great Results
1. **Be Specific:** `Next.js 14` vs `Remix v2` works much better than just `React Frameworks`.
2. **Use "My Case" Intelligently:** The engine is incredibly smart. Tell it your team size, your budget, your fears, and your goals. The more you share about your specific role, the better the Verdict.
3. **Patience is Key:** The beautiful skeleton loader you see means the system is doing heavy multi-agent reasoning. It takes about 10-15 seconds to build a fully structured comparison!
